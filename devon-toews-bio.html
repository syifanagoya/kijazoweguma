Devon Toews | Early Life, Family & Education

Devon Toews was born to his parents, Werner Toews and Tammy Toews, on 21 February 1994 in Abbotsford, British Columbia. Unfortunately, there is not much information regarding his early childhood.

However, what we do know is that before his collegiate days, Devon used to play in the British Columbia Hockey League, where he made a name for himself.

To illustrate, Toews played two seasons for the Surrey Eagles, during which he won the 2013 BCHL Championship and the Western Canada Cup.

Added to that, Toews also earned a selection in the BCHL First All-Star Team and Western Canada Cup All-Tournament Team, along with being named as the Top Defencemen of the 2013 Royal Bank Cup.

All in all, by the time young Devon had to choose a college, he was one of the top young prospects in the country.

Devon Toews |  Career (College & Professional)

Having been one of the top prospects coming into college, it came as no surprise that Devon was offered a full scholarship at Quinnipiac University.

Subsequently, Toews played in 31 games scoring one goal, 17 points, and 16 assists during his freshman year.

Consequently, for his impressive performances, the Canadian-born defenceman earned a nomination in the ECAC Hockey All-Academic Team.

Added to that, Devon was also drafted by the New York Islanders as the 108th overall pick during the 2014 NHL Draft, which made his year even better.

However, the Canadian defenceman decided to continue his development with the Quinnipiac Bobcats as he felt that it was a bit early to be playing in the NHL.

As a result, Toews spent another two years with his university team before making his professional debut.

Devon had won many honors at the culmination of his three years of stay with Quinnipiac University, which propelled his status even further.

To explain, the British Columbia native earned a spot in the ECAC Hockey All-Academic thrice and New England Second Team All-Star once, along with being named in the final list for the ECAC Hockey Best Defensive Defenseman.

Consequently, expectations were high for the Canadian player to make a successful transition into the professional world.

Professional Career

Toews’s professional career began with the team that drafted him in the 2014 NHL Draft, New York Islanders.

After leaving Quinnipiac University, Devon signed a three-year entry-level contract with the Islanders.

Subsequently, he was sent to the Islander’s affiliate team in the American Hockey League (AHL), Bridgeport Sound Tigers.

After that, he spent the next three years honing and developing his physical and mental abilities. Toews’ rookie season with the Tigers was nothing short of extraordinary.

To explain, the Canadian player tied all rookie defencemen in points while also tying the record for most assists by a rookie defenceman with 40.

Additionally, Devon was also selected for the AHL’s All-Star Rookie team and won the fastest skater award at the NHL All-Star Classic with a time of 13.478 seconds.

Consequently, expectations were high for the British Columbia native to perform even better the next season. Likewise, Devon was performing exceedingly well in his second season. 

Due to a shoulder injury sustained against Binghamton Devils, he had to sit out for the remainder of the 2017-18 season.

Nevertheless, his parent franchise, New York Islanders, had seen enough of Toews to grant him a new two-year contract.

Subsequently, Toews started the 2018-19 season with the Tigers but was recalled to the Islanders team after 24 games.

After that, the Canadian defenseman played 48 games for the New York-based team scoring five goals, 18 points, and assisting 13 times.

Devon has appeared 68 times for the Islanders, scoring six goals, 28 points, and assisting a further 22 times.

Further on October 12, 2020, New York Islanders traded Devon to the Colorado Avalanche in exchange for 2021-2022 second-round draft picks.

Devon has improved in all aspects of his game in all seasons and will continue to do so as he is still young.

Thus, we hope that Toews manages to stay injury-free so that he can fulfill his limitless potential.
