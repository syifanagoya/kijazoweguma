Li Keqiang Wiki:- Li Keqiang was born in Hefei, Anhui province on 1 July 1955.  He was a politician and economist from China who led the country as prime minister from 2013 to 2023. In June 1998, at the age of 43, Li was appointed governor of Henan, making him the youngest provincial governor in Chinese history. In December 2004, Li was moved to Liaoning to function as the Party secretary, assuming the position of highest political official in the province. Li became a member of the CCP Politburo Standing Committee during the October 2007 17th Party Congress. Li made his first significant appearance abroad in 2010 at the Davos, Switzerland, World Economic Forum.

Li oversaw the State Council during his tenure and was a key player in China's foreign policy, national security, financial and economic affairs, and deepening reforms.
