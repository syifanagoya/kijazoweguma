Andrew Dice Clay Overview and Wiki

Early Life and Career Beginnings

Andrew Dice Clay, born Andrew Clay Silverstein, began his journey in Brooklyn, New York. Raised in a Jewish household, he found his calling in entertainment early on. Clay’s initial foray into comedy started at Pips Comedy Club, where he honed his skills and developed his unique style.

Early Career and Rise to Fame

Andrew Dice Clay’s journey in the world of comedy began in 1978 at Pips comedy club in Sheepshead Bay, Brooklyn.

Initially focusing on impressions, he soon developed the character The Diceman, inspired by Jerry Lewis’s Buddy Love and John Travolta’s Danny Zuko from Grease. This act led him to perform at prestigious comedy venues like The Improv, Catch a Rising Star, and Dangerfield’s.

Breakthrough and Controversies

In 1980, Clay moved to Los Angeles, performing at The Comedy Store. His first film role came in 1982 with Wacko. By 1983, he had transformed The Diceman into a full-fledged alter ego. His performances at The Comedy Store led to roles on MAS*H and Diff’rent Strokes, as well as films like Making the Grade and Pretty in Pink.

From 1986 to 1988, he appeared regularly on Crime Story.

Clay’s major breakthrough occurred in 1988 after a performance at a Big Brother Association event, leading to a film deal with 20th Century Fox. His appearance on Rodney Dangerfield’s HBO special Nothing Goes Right catapulted him into the national spotlight.

In 1989, his HBO special The Diceman Cometh became a top video rental. His debut comedy album Dice achieved gold certification, selling over 500,000 copies.

However, Clay’s career was not without controversy. His performance at the 1989 MTV Video Music Awards led to a lifetime ban from the network (lifted in 2011). His second album, The Day the Laughter Died, released in 1990, was a commercial success despite its largely improvised content.

Career Milestones

In 1990, Clay made history by selling out Madison Square Garden for two consecutive nights. His appearance on Saturday Night Live that year was marked by protests and controversy. He starred in the film The Adventures of Ford Fairlane in 1990, for which he received a Golden Raspberry Award for Worst Actor.

Later Career and Ventures

In the 1990s, Clay’s career faced challenges. He ran his own production company, Fleebin Dabble Productions, and released the stand-up concert film Dice Rules. However, his controversial material affected its distribution. ABC dropped a proposed television drama featuring him due to his controversial image.

Despite these setbacks, Clay continued to perform. He released the pay-per-view special No Apologies in 1993, which became the highest-grossing non-sports pay-per-view event of the year. He also appeared in more television roles and signed a development deal with CBS, leading to the sitcom Bless This House.

In 1996, Clay returned to his Dice character with the HBO special Assume the Position. He released the triple album Filth in 1998 and appeared on the radio show Opie and Anthony. In 2000, he released I’m Over Here Now and Banned for Life, followed by Face Down, Ass Up.

Recent Endeavors

In the 2000s, Clay attempted a comeback with the VH1 reality TV series Dice: Undisputed and appeared on The Celebrity Apprentice 2. He featured in the eighth season of Entourage and appeared in the Entourage film.

In 2012, he had a stand-up comedy special on Showtime titled Indestructible.

Clay’s podcast Rollin’ with Dice and Wheels…The Podcast began in 2013. He received critical acclaim for his role in Woody Allen’s Blue Jasmine (2013). In 2014, he released his book The Filthy Truth.

He hosted The Blue Show on Showtime in 2015 and appeared in the HBO series Vinyl in 2016.

In 2017, he competed in My Kitchen Rules, and in 2018, he launched the podcast I’m Ova Hea’ Now. His role in A Star is Born (2018) alongside Lady Gaga and Bradley Cooper was well-received.

Personal life

He first tied the knot in 1984 with Kathy Swanson, but their union was short-lived, ending in 1986.

Clay’s second marriage was to Kathleen Monica in 1992. This relationship lasted a decade, ending in 2002, and was notably more enduring than his first.

During this time, Clay and Monica welcomed two sons into their family, Maxwell Lee and Dillon Scott, who have since grown and carved their own paths.

FAQs about Andrew Dice Clay

Who is Andrew Dice Clay?

He is an American stand-up comedian and actor known for his brash and deliberately offensive persona The Diceman.

What made Andrew Dice Clay famous?

Clay gained prominence in the late 1980s with his controversial and edgy humor. His 1989 HBO special The Diceman Cometh and his debut comedy album Dice contributed significantly to his rise in fame.

What was controversial about his performance at the 1989 MTV Video Music Awards?

Clay performed adult versions of Mother Goose nursery rhymes, leading to MTV imposing a lifetime ban on him, which was later lifted in 2011.

What was significant about his performance at Madison Square Garden in 1990?

Clay became the first comedian to sell out two consecutive nights at Madison Square Garden, a milestone in his career.

Why did Nora Dunn and Sinéad O’Connor protest his SNL appearance in 1990?

They objected to his appearance on Saturday Night Live due to the sexist and homophobic content in his stand-up act.

What controversies surrounded his career in the early 1990s?

His controversial act affected his movie Dice Rules with limited theater screenings, and in 1993, ABC dropped a proposed drama featuring him due to his controversial image.

How did he re-emerge in the entertainment industry?

After a period of lower activity, he returned to prominence with acting roles, notably in the TV show Entourage and the movie Blue Jasmine.

Has he been involved in radio or podcasts?

Yes, he has hosted radio shows and made appearances on comedy radio programs, leveraging his distinctive voice and comedic style.

Did he participate in reality TV?

Yes, he appeared in the reality TV series My Kitchen Rules with his wife, Valerie Silverstein, winning the show’s only season.
